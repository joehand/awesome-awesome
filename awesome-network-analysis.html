Books

Classics

  1. A Novitiate in a Period of Change: An Experimental and Case Study of Social Relationships, by Samuel F. Sampson (unpublished PhD dissertation, 1968).
  2. Social Network Analysis. A Handbook, by John Scott (1991, 3rd ed. 2013).
  3. Social Network Analysis. Methods and Applications, by Stanley Wasserman and Katherine Faust (1994).
  4. The Structure and Dynamics of Networks, edited by Mark E.J. Newman, Albert-László Barabási and Duncan J. Watts - 600 pages of classic network analysis articles (2006).

Dissemination

Accessible introductions aimed at non-technical audiences.

  1. Connected: The Surprising Power of Our Social Networks and How They Shape Our Lives, by Nicholas A. Christakis and James H. Fowler (2009).
  2. Linked: The New Science of Networks, by Albert-László Barabási - Selected chapters online (2002).
  3. Network Literacy: Essential Concepts and Core ideas, by the NetSciEd team (c. 2016) - Available in several languages (paper).
  4. Nexus. Small Worlds and the Groundbreaking Theory of Networks, by Mark Buchanan (2003).
  5. Six Degrees: The Science of a Connected Age, by Duncan J. Watts (2003).

General Overviews

  1. Encyclopedia of Social Networks, edited by George A. Barnett - Covers all sorts of network-related themes (many of them not formal) as well as social network analysis (2011).
  2. Encyclopedia of Social Network Analysis and Mining, edited by Reda Alhajj and Jon Rokne (2014).
  3. Network Science, by Albert-László Barabási - Full book online (2016).
  4. Network Science, by the U.S. National Research Council - Full book online (2005).
  5. Network Science: Theory and Practice, by Ted G. Lewis (2011).
  6. Networks. An Introduction, by Mark E.J. Newman (2010).
  7. Networks, Crowds, and Markets: Reasoning About a Highly Connected World, by David Easley and Jon Kleinberg - Full pre-publication draft (review; 2010).
  8. _Réseaux sociaux et structures relationnelles_, by Emmanuel Lazega, in French (2014).
  9. The SAGE Handbook of Social Network Analysis, edited by John Scott and Peter J. Carrington (2011).
  10. Sociologie des réseaux sociaux, by Pierre Mercklé, in French (2011).
  11. Social and Economic Networks, by Matthew O. Jackson (2008).
  12. Social Network Analysis with Applications, by Ian McCulloh, Helen Armstrong and Anthony Johnson (2013).
  13. Social Networks: An Introduction, by Jeroen Bruggeman (related material; 2008).
  14. Studying Social Networks. A Guide to Empirical Research, by Marina Hennig et al. (2013).
  15. Understanding Social Networks. Theories, Concepts, and Findings, by Charles Kadushin (2012).

Graph Theory

  1. Complex Graphs and Networks, by Fan Chung and Linyuan Lu (2006).
  2. The Fascinating World of Graph Theory, Arthur Benjamin, Gary Chartrand and Ping Zhang (2015).
  3. Graph Theory, by Reinhard Diestel - Full book online (2000).
  4. Random Graph Dynamics, by Rick Durrett - Full book online (2007).

Method-specific

  1. Bayesian Networks in R with Applications in Systems Biology, by Radhakrishnan Nagarajan, Marco Scutari and Sophie Lèbre (website; 2013).
  2. Bayesian Networks with Examples in R, by Marco Scutari and Jean-Baptiste Denis (website; 2014).
  3. The Book of Trees. Visualizing Branches of Knowledge, by Manuel Lima - Hundreds of beautiful tree diagrams, from all periods of history (2014).
  4. Exponential Random Graph Models for Social Networks, edited by Dean Lusher, Johan Koskinen and Garry Robins (2013).
  5. Generalized Blockmodeling. Structural Analysis in the Social Sciences, by Patrick Doreian, Vladimir Batagelj and Anuška Ferligoj (2004).
  6. Handbook of Graph Drawing and Visualization, edited by Roberto Tamassia (chapter proofs; 2013).
  7. Handbuch Historische Netzwerkforschung. Grundlagen und Anwendungen, edited by Marten Düring et al., in German (2016).
  8. An Introduction to Exponential Random Graph Modeling, by Jenine K. Harris (2014).
  9. Knoten und Kanten. Soziale Netzwerkanalyse in Wirtschafts- und Migrationsforschung, edited by Markus Gamper and Linda Reschke, in German (2010).
  10. Knoten und Kanten 2.0. Soziale Netzwerkanalyse in Medienforschung und Kulturanthropologie, edited by Markus Gamper, Linda Reschke and Michael Schönhuth, in German (2012).
  11. Knoten und Kanten III. Soziale Netzwerkanalyse in Geschichts- und Politikforschung, edited by Markus Gamper, Linda Reschke and Marten Düring, in German and English (2015).
  12. Inferential Network Analysis, by Skyler J. Cranmer, Bruce A. Desmarais and Jason Morgan (forthcoming).
  13. Multilevel Network Analysis for the Social Sciences, edited by Emmanuel Lazega and Tom A.B. Snijders (2016).
  14. Multivariate Network Visualization, edited by Andreas Kerren, Helen C. Purchase and Matthew O. Ward (2014).
  15. Network Analysis in Archaeology, edited by Carl Knappett (2013; review in French).
  16. Network Analysis: Methodological Foundations, edited by Ulrik Brandes and Thomas Erlebach - Covers network centrality, clustering, blockmodels, spatial networks and more (2005).
  17. Political Networks. The Structural Perspective, by David Knoke (1994).
  18. Social Network Analysis for Ego-Nets: Social Network Analysis for Actor-Centred Networks, by Nick Crossley et al. (2015).
  19. Understanding Large Temporal Networks and Spatial Networks, by Vladimir Batagelj et al. (2014).

Software-specific

  1. Analyzing Social Networks (using UCINET), by Stephen P. Borgatti, Martin G. Everett and Jeffrey C. Johnson (2013).
  2. A User's Guide to Network Analysis in R, by Douglas A. Luke (2015).
  3. Exploratory Social Network Analysis with Pajek, by Wouter de Nooy, Andrej Mrvar and Vladimir Batagelj (2011; also in Japanese and in Chinese).
  4. Gephi Cookbook (2015).
  5. Graph Drawing Software (covering many programs), edited by Michael Jünger and Petra Mutzel (2004).
  6. Introduction to Social Network Methods (using mostly UCINET), by Robert A. Hanneman and Mark Riddle - Full book online (2001).
  7. Mastering Gephi Network Visualization, by Ken Cherven (2015).
  8. Network Analysis with R/igraph, by Gabor Csárdi, Thomas Nepusz and Eduardo M. Airoldi (in preparation).
  9. Network Analysis with Python/igraph, by Thomas Nepusz, Gabor Csárdi and Eduardo M. Airoldi (in preparation).
  10. Network Graph Analysis and Visualization with Gephi, by Ken Cherven (2013).
  11. Social Network Analysis for Startups. Finding Connections on the Social Web (using Python), by Maksim Tsvetovat and Alexander Kouznetsov (code; 2011).
  12. Statistical Analysis of Network Data with R, by Eric D. Kolaczyk and Gabor Csárdi (R package; 2014).

Topic-specific

  1. Communities and Networks: Using Social Network Analysis to Rethink Urban and Community Studies, by Katherine Giuffre (2013).
  2. Comparing Policy Networks. Labor Politics in the U.S., Germany, and Japan, by David Knoke et al. (1996).
  3. The Connected Past. Challenges to Network Studies in Archaeology and History edited by Tom Brughmans, Anna Collar and Fiona Coward (2016; companion website).
  4. The Development of Social Network Analysis: A Study in the Sociology of Science, by Linton C. Freeman, in English and several other languages (2004; follow-up paper, 2011).
  5. Dynamical Processes on Complex Networks, by Alain Barrat, Marc Barthélemy and Alessandro Vespignani (2008).
  6. Inside Criminal Networks, by Carlo Morselli (2009).
  7. Neighbor Networks. Competitive Advantage Local and Personal, by Ronald S. Burt (2010).
  8. Networks in Social Policy Problems, edited by Balázs Vedres and Marco Scotti (2012).
  9. The Oxford Handbook of the Economics of Networks, edited by Yann Bramoullé, Andrea Galeotti and Brian Rogers (2016).
  10. _Policy Debates as Dynamic Networks: German Pension Politics and Privatization Discourse_, by Philip Leifeld (2016).
  11. Small Worlds: The Dynamics of Networks between Order and Randomness, by Duncan J. Watts (2003).
  12. Theories of Communication Networks, by Peter Monge and Nosh Contractor (2003).
  13. Towards Relational Sociology, by Nick Crossley (2011).
  14. Die Verbundenheit der Dinge. Eine Kulturgeschichte der Netze und Netzwerke [The Connectedness of Things. A Cultural History of Nets and Networks], by Sebastian Gießmann, in German (2014).
  15. Verdeckte soziale Netzwerke im Nationalsozialismus. Die Entstehung und Arbeitsweise von Berliner Hilfsnetzwerken für verfolgte Juden [Hidden Social Networks in National Socialism: The origins and working methods of Berlin assistance networks for persecuted Jews], by Marten Düring, in German (2015; related publications and video presentation in English).
  16. _Visualisierung komplexer Strukturen. Grundlagen der Darstellung mehrdimensionaler Netzwerke_, by Lothar Krempel, in German.

  16. Manlio De Domenico’s Multilayer Networks.
  17. Mark E.J. Newman’s Network Data (example visualizations).
  18. Network Repository - Fully searchable database containing hundreds of real-world networks.

  20. Norwegian Interlocking Directorate, 2002-2011 - Two-mode and one-mode data on gender representation in Norwegian firms.
  21. Pajek Datasets.
  22. Philosophers Networks from Randall Collins's The Sociology of Philosophies.
  23. Siena Datasets.
  24. SocioPatterns Datasets - Network data obtained through the SocioPatterns sensing platform.

Selected Papers

A voluntarily short list of applied, epistemological and methodological articles, many of which have become classic readings in network analysis courses. Intended for highly motivated social science students with little to no prior exposure to network analysis.

  1. Aux sources des grands réseaux d’interactions. Retour sur quelques propriétés déterminantes des réseaux sociaux issus de corpus documentaires, by Pascal Cristofoli, in French - Reviews the current state of relational sociology and network analysis in light of the large-scale and online data (Réseaux, 2008).
  2. Birds of a Feather, Or Friend of a Friend? Using Exponential Random Graph Models to Investigate Adolescent Social Networks, by Steven M. Goodreau, James A. Kitts and Martina Morris - Accessible introduction to the logic and application of exponential random graph modeling (Demography, 2001).
  3. Chains of Affection: The Structure of Adolescent Romantic and Sexual Networks, by Peter S. Bearman, James Moody and Katherine Stovel - Classic example of topological network analysis applied to a network of affective and sexual ties (American Journal of Sociology, 2004).
  4. Coauthorship and Citation Patterns in the Physical Review, by Travis Martin et al. - Highly typical study of scientific publishing productivity and collaboration through temporal network analysis (preprint; Physical Review E, 2013).
  5. The Convergence of Social and Technological Networks, by Jon Kleinberg - Discusses small-world effects and social contagion within the context of the Internet and social media (Communications of the ACM, 2008).
  6. Deux traditions d'analyse des reseaux sociaux, by Michael Eve (English version; Réseaux, 2002).
  7. Homophily and Contagion Are Generically Confounded in Observational Social Network Studies, by Cosma R. Shalizi and Andrew C. Thomas - Makes a very important point for the analysis of network diffusion and influence (Sociological Methods and Research, 2011).
  8. La notion de réseau complexe : du réseau comme abstraction et outil à la masse de données des réseaux sociaux en ligne, by Alain Barrat, in French - Accessible introduction to the study of complex networks (Communication & Organisation, 2013).
  9. Network Analysis, Culture, and the Problem of Agency, by Mustafa Emirbayer and Jeff Goodwin (American Journal of Sociology, 1994), and Manifesto for a Relational Sociology, by Mustafa Emirbayer (American Journal of Sociology, 1997) - Sociological foundations for a science of social ties.
  10. Network Theory, Plot Analysis, by Franco Moretti - Example applications of (fictional) network analysis in literary studies (New Left Review, 2011).
  11. Node Centrality in Weighted Networks: Generalizing Degree and Shortest Paths, by Tore Opsahl, Filip Agneessens and John Skvoretz - Explores the generalization of network centrality and distance measures to (positively) valued graphs (Social Networks, 2010; companion website).
  12. Scale-Free Networks, by Albert-László Barabási and Eric Bonabeau - Early, accessible formulation of the “networks are everywhere” argument (Scientific American, 2003).
  13. Social Networks and Causal Inference, by Tyler J. VanderWeele and Weihua An - Reviews the different ways in which network analysis can produce meaningful causal statements, as well as the inherent limits of network analysis for doing so (Handbook of Causal Analysis for Social Research, 2013).
  14. The Performativity of Networks, by Kieran Healy - Network analysis meets science studies: social networks, like financial markets, are highly subject to performativity, i.e. the possibility that reality might be altered by its theoretical inquiry (European Journal of Sociology, 2015).
  15. Robust Action and the Rise of the Medici, 1400-1434, by John F. Padgett and Christopher K. Ansell - Classic analysis of power relations in the Renaissance Florentine state (American Journal of Sociology, 1993).
  16. The Strength of Weak Ties, by Mark Granovetter - Arch-classic example of applying network analysis to a social issue: jobseeking (American Journal of Sociology, 1973).
  17. The Ties that Divide: A Network Analysis of the International Monetary System, 1890–1910 (The Journal of Economic History, 2005) and The Empirics of International Currencies: Network Externalities, History and Persistence (The Economic Journal, 2009), both by Marc Flandreau and Clemens Jobst - Network analysis of the foreign exchange system in the late 19th century (data).
  18. Topics in Social Network Analysis and Network Science, by A. James O'Malley and Jukka-Pekka Onnela - 50-page introduction to network analysis, with just the right amount of detail on all aspects of it (The Handbook of Health Services Research, forthcoming 2017).
